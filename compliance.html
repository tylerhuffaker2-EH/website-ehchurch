Technology Ministry

Compliance Requirements

This page outlines the policies and standards the Technology Ministry must follow as set forth by Everlasting Hope Church.

Overview

What compliance means for us

As part of the church's ministry efforts, all technological projects, content, and services are expected to uphold Biblical integrity, protect users, and adhere to applicable laws. The following requirements provide a framework for responsible development and operation within the Technology Ministry.

Key Requirements

Standards we observe

  • Respect for intellectual property and copyright; no unauthorized use of third-party assets.
  • Protection of personal data and privacy in accordance with church policies and legal regulations (e.g. GDPR, COPPA where applicable).
  • Child safety protocols for any youth-facing tools, including moderation and reporting mechanisms.
  • Accessibility considerations to ensure content is usable by people with disabilities.
  • Security best practices, such as using HTTPS, secure authentication, and regular audits.
  • Content review for doctrinal consistency and avoidance of material contrary to church teachings.
  • Clear disclaimer that tech tools are provided as ministry resources, not professional advice.

These items are reviewed periodically by church leadership and updated as needed.

Development Standards

Technology Ministry rules

These standards apply to all software projects created, maintained, or distributed through the Technology Ministry of Everlasting Hope Church. They operate in addition to all existing church policies and governance, and they exist to ensure projects reflect integrity, transparency, security, and responsible stewardship.

  1. Ad-Free Software Policy
    All software produced under the Technology Ministry must remain free from advertising networks, third-party promotional placements, tracking advertisements, or monetized ad systems. Applications developed through the ministry must prioritize ministry purpose, user well-being, and ethical design over advertising revenue or engagement manipulation.
  2. Artificial Intelligence Disclosure
    If artificial intelligence tools are used at any stage of development—including but not limited to coding assistance, documentation support, design suggestions, debugging, or testing—this use must be clearly disclosed within the project’s development or information section. AI may be used as a development aid, but it does not replace human review, accountability, or responsibility for the final product. All final software must be reviewed and approved by human developers or ministry leadership before release.
  3. Developer Attribution
    All individuals who contribute to the development of a software project must be appropriately credited within the project’s development information section. This may include lead developers, contributing developers, designers, technical advisors, and ministry oversight; proper attribution ensures transparency, accountability, and recognition for contributors.
  4. Sensitive Project Security
    Certain software projects may contain sensitive internal systems, ministry operations, internal documentation, or protected technical structures. Projects determined to be sensitive must be maintained as closed-source software and may not have their source code publicly released. Examples include internal ministry management tools, infrastructure systems, platforms containing protected internal architecture, and applications designated sensitive by church leadership (such as RootedLife). The final determination of sensitivity is made by church leadership.
  5. Oversight and Accountability
    All technology projects remain subject to review, oversight, and approval by Everlasting Hope Church leadership. The President of the church, or designated leadership, retains authority to classify projects as public or sensitive, approve releases, establish security requirements, and enforce compliance with ministry policies. Projects exist to serve the mission of the church and must remain accountable to leadership.
  6. Data Privacy and Responsible Data Use
    Software developed under the Technology Ministry must respect user privacy and responsible data stewardship. Applications should only collect data necessary for operation or ministry purpose. Whenever possible, sensitive personal information should not be collected; data should be stored securely; and users should be informed about what data is collected and why. Developers must take reasonable steps to protect user data from unauthorized access or misuse. Any software collecting personal information must be reviewed for appropriate privacy practices before release.
  7. Volunteer Developer Agreements
    Individuals who contribute to ministry technology projects as volunteers must agree that their work is contributed for the benefit of Everlasting Hope Church. Volunteer contributors acknowledge that their contributions may be modified or maintained by other developers, become part of the ministry project, and that church leadership retains final authority over project direction and distribution. This ensures continuity of development and protects ministry projects from disruption.
  8. Code Ownership and Continuity
    All software developed under the Technology Ministry is considered a ministry project of Everlasting Hope Church. To ensure long-term sustainability, source code and project infrastructure must remain accessible to church leadership; no single developer should maintain exclusive control; and development environments and repositories should be organized to allow future maintenance. If a developer leaves the ministry, the project must remain maintainable by others or leadership.
  9. Security and Access Control
    Technology Ministry projects must follow basic security practices to protect church systems and user information. This includes limiting administrative access to authorized individuals, protecting credentials and authentication systems, avoiding public exposure of sensitive systems, and maintaining secure storage of project infrastructure. Security decisions should prioritize protecting ministry resources and user trust.
  10. Alignment With Ministry Mission
    All software created through the Technology Ministry must support the mission and values of Everlasting Hope Church. Technology should serve as a tool for spiritual growth, ministry outreach, community support, and operational efficiency. Projects that conflict with the church’s mission or ethical standards may be discontinued or restructured.
